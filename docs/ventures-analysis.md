# SafeguardJunub / TAMAMHEALTH — Evidence-Based Venture Analysis

## Executive Summary

TAMAMHEALTH is a digital health records system designed for South Sudan's health infrastructure. This document presents the evidence-based case for the platform, grounded in primary research from *The Lancet Primary Care* (2025). The evidence overwhelmingly supports three conclusions: (1) primary care is the backbone of health-care delivery globally and digital systems are essential to its effectiveness; (2) community health workers are among the most cost-effective interventions available for low-resource settings; and (3) EHR systems that surface clinical decision support, flag diagnostic delays, and track treatment continuity can materially reduce mortality and health inequities — exactly the problems TAMAMHEALTH was built to solve.

## 1. The Problem: Primary Care Under Pressure

### 1.1 Mental Health Burden on Primary Care

- Across 11 countries, **1,059,101,897 primary care visits** were analysed; of these, **49,022,341 (4.6%) were attributed to anxiety or depression** among adults aged 20+.
  *[Source: Flehr et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100070]*

- Anxiety-related and depression-related consultations **rose sharply during the COVID-19 pandemic and remained elevated** in the post-pandemic recovery period in Canada, China, Peru, Sweden, and the USA.
  *[Source: Lukaschek, Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100090]*

- **15% of the world's population** had mental health disorders in 2023, with depressive and anxiety disorders being the most burdensome. Estimates suggest that if optimal treatments were available, **71% of the global anxiety disorder burden could be avoided**.
  *[Source: Editorial, Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100097]*

- Only **17 of 138 responding countries (12%)** met all five WHO criteria for functional integration of mental health into primary care (guidelines available, pharmacological treatment available, psychosocial interventions provided, primary care staff trained, and involvement of mental health specialists).
  *[Source: Editorial, Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100097 — citing WHO Mental Health Atlas 2024]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** South Sudan's health system has minimal mental health infrastructure. TAMAMHEALTH's consultation module can integrate mental health screening at every clinical encounter, enabling the kind of routine surveillance that this research shows is critical.

### 1.2 Diagnostic Delays Kill — EHR Systems Can Prevent Them

- In a national study of **412,173 adults** newly diagnosed with heart failure in England (2000–2021), **44.0% (181,287) were first diagnosed during emergency hospital admission** — with substantially worse survival.
  *[Source: Lawson et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100060]*

- Among those with prior symptoms or diuretic use, **63.8% (175,041 of 274,228) had none of the recommended diagnostic investigations** conducted in primary care before diagnosis.
  *[Source: Lawson et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100060]*

- Diagnostic lag times **worsened** from a median of 16.4 months (2000–04) to **35.4 months** (2015–19) between the first recorded symptom and heart failure diagnosis.
  *[Source: Lawson et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100060]*

- Patients first diagnosed as inpatients with long-term loop diuretic use but **no previous primary care investigation** had an adjusted hazard ratio of **5.29 (95% CI 4.83–5.79)** for 5-year mortality, compared with outpatients who had primary care diagnostic pathways.
  *[Source: Lawson et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100060]*

- **Women, people in deprived areas, and those with multimorbidity** were significantly less likely to receive diagnostic investigations or be diagnosed as outpatients.
  *[Source: Lawson et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100060]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** TAMAMHEALTH's AI diagnosis engine, clinical decision support alerts, and structured consultation workflow directly address the problem of missed investigations. In a setting like South Sudan — where specialist referral is often impossible — the system's ability to flag symptom patterns and recommend diagnostic steps at the point of care is a potential lifesaver.

### 1.3 Treatment Continuity in Low-Resource Settings

- In a cohort of **68,888 adults** on antiretroviral therapy (ART) in the Western Cape, South Africa, the cumulative incidence of treatment interruption (>90 days late) by 6 years was **71.8% (95% CI 71.4–72.3)**.
  *[Source: Keene et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100077]*

- **65.8% (45,310)** of patients had at least one substantial treatment interruption during follow-up.
  *[Source: Keene et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100077]*

- Among those who interrupted and returned, only **50.3% were virally suppressed** 1 year after re-engagement, compared with **96.7% of those continuously in care**.
  *[Source: Keene et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100077]*

- The hazard of interruption was **highest in the first 6 months** after ART initiation and after restart, emphasising the critical early-engagement window.
  *[Source: Keene et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100077]*

- The study concludes: "Health systems need to consider the dynamics of long-term engagement in designing services that acknowledge people dropping out of care as normal, accommodating rather than penalising disengagement, and making it easy for people to return to care as soon as they are able."
  *[Source: Keene et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100077]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** TAMAMHEALTH's follow-up tracking, patient messaging, and boma health worker visit modules are precisely the tools needed to maintain treatment continuity and facilitate re-engagement. The system should be enhanced with automated alerts for missed appointments, particularly in the critical first 6 months of any chronic treatment.

## 2. The Opportunity: Community Health Workers as Force Multipliers

- A systematic review of **130 studies and 380 cost-effectiveness scenarios** found that community health worker (CHW) programmes were cost-effective in:
  - **78–93%** of reproductive, maternal, newborn, and child health (RMNCH) scenarios
  - **81–85%** of NCD and mental health scenarios
  - **83–88%** of HIV, tuberculosis, and malaria scenarios
  *[Source: Stansert Katzen et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100076]*

- Returns on CHW investment are worth **up to 10 times** the investment in terms of improved health outcomes and averted health-care costs.
  *[Source: Stansert Katzen et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100076]*

- The **median annual cost per capita** for CHW programmes, standardised to 2024 USD, was **US$0.59**, with costs per beneficiary of just **$10.03**.
  *[Source: Stansert Katzen et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100076]*

- **Integrated approaches** — linking CHWs to primary care facilities, using digital tools, formalising employment, or running multidisease campaigns — tend to be **more cost-effective than standalone models** focused on a single disease.
  *[Source: Stansert Katzen et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100076]*

- CHW-led interventions for HIV, tuberculosis, and malaria in Liberia **cut diagnostic and treatment costs for neglected tropical diseases by up to 10 times** compared with single-disease approaches.
  *[Source: Stansert Katzen et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100076]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** TAMAMHEALTH's boma health worker module is a direct implementation of the CHW model shown to deliver the highest returns. The system's design — equipping CHWs with digital tools linked to the primary care facility network — mirrors the "integrated approach" that this research identifies as most cost-effective.

## 3. The Evidence for EHR-Based Interventions

### 3.1 Electronic Health Records Enable Population-Level Research

Multiple Lancet articles used large-scale EHR databases to generate actionable clinical insights:

- The **Clinical Practice Research Datalink (CPRD)** in England covers approximately **20% of the English population** and was the data source for both the heart failure diagnosis study (412,173 patients) and the corticosteroid prescribing study (2,564,729 patients).
  *[Source: Lawson et al., 2025; Whittaker et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100080]*

- The corticosteroid study identified **12,029,952 individual prescriptions** to 2,564,729 patients in a single year (2023), with **16.1% of the population** receiving at least one corticosteroid. Nearly half (49.4%) had a recorded clinical indication.
  *[Source: Whittaker et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100080]*

- The INTRePID consortium aggregated **over 1 billion primary care visits** across 11 countries to track mental health trends — possible only because participating countries had robust EHR infrastructure.
  *[Source: Flehr et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100070]*

- South Africa's **Provincial Health Data Centre** in the Western Cape consolidated data from **52 hospitals and 354 primary care clinics** covering an estimated **8 million patients** — enabling the ART treatment interruption analysis.
  *[Source: Keene et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100077]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** TAMAMHEALTH creates the foundational data infrastructure that makes this kind of analysis possible for South Sudan. Without structured digital health records, population-level health insights remain invisible.

### 3.2 Prescribing Patterns Require Monitoring

- **16.1% of the English population** received at least one corticosteroid prescription in 2023 via primary care.
  *[Source: Whittaker et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100080]*

- The most common indications were asthma (801,924 [63.3%]), eczema (229,382 [18.1%]), and COPD (220,759 [17.4%]).
  *[Source: Whittaker et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100080]*

- Among patients on oral corticosteroids, **71.9% had a clinical indication recorded within 2 years**, but a substantial minority did not.
  *[Source: Whittaker et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100080]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** TAMAMHEALTH's pharmacy module tracks all prescriptions. This Lancet evidence shows the importance of linking prescriptions to documented clinical indications — a feature TAMAMHEALTH's structured consultation-to-prescription workflow already supports.

### 3.3 AI in Clinical Decision Support

- AI detection systems for health misinformation achieve accuracy rates ranging from **77% to more than 85%**, though counter-evidence for novel false claims is often unavailable.
  *[Source: Yu, Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100087 — citing Nasir et al., 2021]*

- **94.2% of health-care practitioners** reported encountering patient misinformation in the previous year, yet most had **not received relevant training** in misinformation management.
  *[Source: Yu, Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100087 — citing Wood et al., 2021]*

- Health-care systems might benefit from integrating "decision-support tools, real-time misinformation alerts, and AI-assisted patient education resources."
  *[Source: Yu, Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100087]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** TAMAMHEALTH's offline AI diagnosis engine, clinical scribe, and medical assistant chatbot align with this evidence. The system provides WHO/IMCI-based clinical decision support at the point of care, counteracting misinformation and supporting evidence-based practice.

## 4. Maternity Care: The Relational Imperative

- A scoping review of 175 reports found that **only one country (New Zealand)** has successfully scaled up continuity of midwifery care at a national level.
  *[Source: Lavender et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100069 — citing Bradford et al., 2022]*

- In many LMICs, a "tick-box culture" prevails where health workers must address each item on an antenatal card within restricted time slots, resulting in **transactional care** that leaves women feeling undervalued.
  *[Source: Lavender et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100069]*

- Relational care was provided **discriminately**, with health workers displaying "affinity bias" — only providing relational care to individuals they could identify with (same tribe, education, or social status).
  *[Source: Lavender et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100069 — citing Lavender et al., BMJ Glob Health 2021]*

- Women in Ethiopia were **unwilling to reveal psychosocial issues** with health workers who did not communicate empathically, directly affecting quality of antenatal assessments.
  *[Source: Lavender et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100069 — citing Eshetu et al., 2025]*

- Negative maternity experiences are shared within communities and **deter others from antenatal engagement**.
  *[Source: Lavender et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100069 — citing Finlayson & Downe, PLoS Med 2013]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** TAMAMHEALTH's ANC module follows the WHO 8-contact protocol. The evidence here argues for going beyond checklist completion. The system should support relational care by capturing patient preferences, psychosocial risk factors, and communication notes — enabling every health worker who encounters a patient to provide continuity of information even when continuity of carer is not possible.

## 5. Workforce and Access: Structural Solutions

### 5.1 Rural Health Workforce Retention

- France's TUS-SAM model (Territorial University of Health) achieved a **79% physician retention rate** in underserved areas since 2009 through shared hospital-university fellowships.
  *[Source: Levaillant et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100075]*

- A 2024 survey found **85% of students were highly satisfied** with rural placements, and **47% expressed intention to practice in rural or semi-rural areas**.
  *[Source: Levaillant et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100075]*

- Decentralised teaching doubled placement growth (from 54 to 78 in Mayenne, 198 to 263 in Sarthe between 2019 and 2023).
  *[Source: Levaillant et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100075]*

### 5.2 Advanced Practice Nursing

- The transformative potential of advanced practice nurses (APNs) in primary care spans **four domains**: regulatory frameworks, education, service redesign, and public campaigns.
  *[Source: Schlegel et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100083]*

- APNs are critical for LMICs where physician shortages are acute, but implementation requires sustainable funding and integration into routine care.
  *[Source: Schlegel et al., Lancet Prim Care 2025; 1: 100083]*

**Relevance to TAMAMHEALTH:** TAMAMHEALTH's role-based access control (11 roles including clinical officers, nurses, and community health workers) reflects the task-shifting model this research advocates. The system enables non-physician providers to deliver structured, guideline-based care.

## 8. Unsupported Claims — Flags

The following claims are commonly made about digital health systems in low-resource settings but **lack direct supporting evidence from the sources reviewed**:

1. **"EHR adoption reduces medication errors by X%"** — No direct statistic from these Lancet articles. The corticosteroid study (Whittaker et al.) shows the importance of tracking prescriptions against indications, but does not quantify error reduction from EHR adoption specifically.

2. **"Digital health records reduce patient wait times"** — Not addressed in any of the 13 articles.

3. **"Offline-first systems improve health outcomes compared to paper records"** — While the ART study (Keene et al.) relied on routine digital health data from primary care clinics, no study directly compares offline-first digital systems to paper records.

4. **"AI clinical decision support reduces misdiagnosis rates"** — The ICE-I commentary (Yu) cites 77–85% accuracy for misinformation detection, but no study in this collection measures AI CDS impact on misdiagnosis in clinical settings.

5. **"Mobile health apps improve CHW effectiveness"** — The CHW cost-effectiveness review (Stansert Katzen et al.) notes that integrated approaches using digital tools are more cost-effective, but does not isolate the mobile-app component.

These claims may be supportable from other literature but should not be attributed to these Lancet sources.
